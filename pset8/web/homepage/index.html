Oceans

Introduction

The oceans cover a vast area of the surface of our planet - about 70% and are home to the majority of the biomes of the world. As Sir David Attenborough notes in the documentary 'The Blue Planet' - 'We know more about the surface of the moon than the bottom of the oceans'. More than eighty percernt of the oceans are unmaped, unexplored and unobserved. Life in oceans if very different than life on land and may efforts have been made to study the organisms that inhabitat in the oceans and observe their impact on the ecosystems and the human life. From the coral reefs in the shallows ideal for snorkelling to the deepest points on the earth where the pressures can crush humans like a soda can - life exists everywhere and most of it is unobserved. Today, we take a moment from our busy lives and see a few of the interesting creatures that live in the deep blue waters.
