Crown Jellyfish

Crown Jellyfish are invertibrates and have a very flexible body. They can grow large very fast because their bodies contain upto ninety five percent (95%) water [1]. Crown jellyfish are able to make light through bioluminescence. When they are touched, their bells will light up. Otherwise, the bell of a crown jellyfish will look transparent when undisturbed. When they are attacked, crown jellyfish are able to startle, mislead, and distract their predators with the light that they produce. They may also use their bioluminescence to lure or dazzle their prey [2]. Jellyfishes do not have a brain, or blood, or bones or even a nervous system; they have a rudimentary senses on their tentacles which, primarily, have only one job - they inform the jellyfish when it is touching some organism which it then devours. Despite this they have not only survived but thrived generation after generation for millions of years [3]. Crown Jellyfish are of eight different species but they are known due to the crown like shape of their head. They are known to be venomous and can sting through their tentacles.
