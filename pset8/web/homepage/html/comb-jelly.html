Comb Jelly

Comb Jellies are jellyfish like creatures that do not have a brain, blood, bones or even a nervous system. Their bodies are made primarily of water up to 95% - due to which they can grow in size rapidly.Their body contains of a jelly like membrane which has some sensory qualities and they react to touch by either predators or the prey alike. They float around in the currents and open their bag like mouth opening and consume anything that touches its edge. They also are notable for their extremely flambouyant display of colors which is used to attract prey. They are notable for the groups of cilia they use for swimming (commonly referred to as "combs"), and they are the largest animals to swim with the help of cilia. Depending on the species, adult ctenophores range from a few millimeters to 1.5 m (4 ft 11 in) in size. Only 100 to 150 species have been validated, and possibly another 25 have not been fully described and named.[1]
