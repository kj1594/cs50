Stygiomedusa Gigantea

Stygiomedusa Gigantea is one of the largest known invertibrate predator found in the ocean. It does not have tentacles but instead it has a long cloth like thin membrane called medusa that suspend from its body. These long brown-maroon coloured medusa can grow up to twelve meters (12 m) or thirty-six feet (36 ft) in length which is extraordinary. The medusa float in the water like a cloth suspended inside the water and catch smaller beings like a net. Stygiomedusae can grow up to one hundred and fouty centimeters (140 cm) in diameter but most specimen found in the wild ranged from fifty to one hunderd centimeters (50-100 cm). More surprisingly though, stygiomedusae are extremely rare to encounter. Reportedly, they have only been sighted a hundred and fourteen (114) times in the past one hundred and ten (110) years - making them one of the most rare beings on the planet. They are generally found at depths greater than nine hundred meters (900 m) all the way up to eighteen hundred meters (1800 m).
